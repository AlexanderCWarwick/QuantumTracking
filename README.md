# Quantum optimization for particle tracking problems

In this project the application of quantum computing solutions to the problem of particle tracking is explored. Such solutions are invaluable in high-energy particle experiments, such as the [LHCb](https://home.cern/science/experiments/lhcb/) experiment at [CERN](https://home.cern/) as they allow the identification and parameterisation of final-state particles. Quantum optimisation of such combinatorially complex problem may prove better than classical benchmarks.


![LHCb Proton Tracks](Images/ProtonCollisionTracks.jpg)

*Image from LHCb experiment, CERN.*


For the purposes of simulating quantum circuits, the project uses the [Qiskit](https://github.com/Qiskit) and [Quiskit-aer](https://github.com/Qiskit/qiskit-aer) libraries.
